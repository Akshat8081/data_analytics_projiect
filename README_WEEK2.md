# 📈 Exploratory Data Analysis Performed

## 1️⃣ Descriptive Statistics

Calculated the following statistical measures for **Total Price**:

- Mean
- Median
- Trim Mean
- Standard Deviation
- Minimum
- Maximum
- First Quartile (Q1)
- Third Quartile (Q3)
- Interquartile Range (IQR)
- Lower Bound
- Upper Bound

---

## 2️⃣ Outlier Detection

Used the **Interquartile Range (IQR)** method to detect potential outliers.

### Formula

Upper Bound = Q3 + (1.5 × IQR)

Lower Bound = Q1 − (1.5 × IQR)

Orders outside these limits were considered potential outliers.

---

## 3️⃣ Correlation Analysis

Performed correlation analysis between:

- Quantity vs Total Price
- Unit Price vs Total Price

### Findings

- Quantity and Total Price show a **moderate positive correlation (r = 0.615)**.
- Unit Price and Total Price show a **strong positive correlation (r = 0.717)**.

Scatter plots were created to visualize these relationships.

# 🔍 Key Insights

- Chair generated the highest revenue.
- Phone generated the lowest revenue.
- Credit Card contributed the highest revenue among payment methods.
- June recorded the highest number of orders.
- September recorded the lowest number of orders.
- Customer spending varies considerably across transactions.
- High-value orders create positive skewness in Total Price.
- Unit Price has a stronger impact on Total Price than Quantity.
- Coupon usage is fairly balanced across customers.
- Potential outliers were identified using the IQR method.
